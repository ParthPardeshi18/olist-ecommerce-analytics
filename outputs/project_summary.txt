  At threshold: 65% recall, 14.2% precision on late orders

TOP 5 BUSINESS INSIGHTS
  1. SELLER CONCENTRATION RISK: Gini coefficient of 0.79 — the top 18% of
     sellers generate 80% of revenue. Losing a handful of top performers
     could devastate platform GMV. Seller development programs for mid-tier
     sellers are essential for revenue diversification.

  2. DELIVERY SCALABILITY BOTTLENECK: On-time delivery rate dropped from
     95.5% (H1 2017) to 90.8% (2018), with a significant negative correlation
     (r=-0.54) between order volume and on-time rate. The platform is
     growing faster than its logistics capacity — this will compound.

  3. REVIEW SCORE DAMAGE FROM DELAYS: Late delivery costs an average of
     1.67 review stars (4.22 on-time vs 2.55 late). Orders >14 days late
     average just 1.61 stars. At 8,317 late orders, this represents
     massive reputational damage and likely suppresses repeat purchases.

  4. RIO DE JANEIRO RETENTION RISK: RJ is the 2nd-highest revenue state
     (R$2.5M+) but has the worst late delivery rate among top-5 states
     at 13.5%. This mismatch between revenue importance and delivery
     quality makes RJ the highest-priority market for logistics investment.

  5. SP SUPPLY CHAIN DOMINANCE: SP sellers fulfill 70.8% of ALL orders
     nationally, and the SP-to-SP route alone accounts for 31.8% of
     order volume. This geographic concentration creates efficiency but
     also a single-point-of-failure risk for the entire marketplace.

KEY RECOMMENDATIONS BY PHASE
  Phase 2 (Revenue):   Promote 6+ installment credit card options —
                        they drive 39% of CC revenue
  Phase 3 (Delivery):  Invest in logistics for Northern states (RR, AM, PA)

  Phase 4 (Geography): Develop seller bases in MG and PR to diversify
                        supply chain away from SP dependency
  Phase 5 (Segments):  Champions (29.6% of customers) generate 57.3% of

  Phase 6 (Model):     Deploy XGBoost predictor to flag ~1,787 at-risk
